Carsharing app — Increasing first trip conversion

Overview

Getmancar is a carsharing service operating across Ukraine, Georgia and Moldova. Users find a car on the map, unlock it in the app, and pay per minute, hour or day — no paperwork, no keys.

Research

We rode the cars ourselves, interviewed users, and analysed competitors. The key insight: users weren't failing because of bad intentions — they were failing because the app never gave them a reason to stay.

1. Tariff selection as a separate step

Users saw all tariff information dumped onto the car card — prices, conditions, deposits — with no way to understand which tariff made sense for their trip. Confusion led to drop-off.

Before
After

Hypothesis

If we separate tariff selection into its own step and let users calculate the cost based on their destination, more users will feel confident enough to book their first trip.

Simplified tariff selection

  • Removed excessive information from car cards
  • Moved tariff choice into a dedicated step
  • Displayed full tariff details on a separate screen

2. Verification after car selection

The flow forced verification right after sign-up, interrupting users before starting a trip. Lack of clarity around timing and next steps reduced motivation, causing drop-off.

Hypothesis

If we move verification after users choose a car and a tariff, and make the process more transparent with clear feedback and expectations, more users will feel motivated to complete verification and proceed to their first trip.

Verification triggered after car selection

  • Sign-in appears after the user selects a car and tariff
  • Users reach verification with clear intent to start a trip
  • Reduced drop-off at the identity verification step

Streamlined document upload flow

  • Licence scan with clear progress indication
  • Car reserved during document review — no risk of losing the booking
  • Motion design used to reduce perceived wait time
Metrics we'd track: Verification completion rate · First ride conversion · Drop-off during verification

3. Solving the "no cars nearby" problem

Almost half of verified users fail to start their first trip due to no nearby cars. Many drop off and switch to competitors.
The product doesn't support users at this critical moment.

Hypothesis

If we proactively notify users when a car becomes available nearby, we can increase return rate and drive more first and repeat bookings.
